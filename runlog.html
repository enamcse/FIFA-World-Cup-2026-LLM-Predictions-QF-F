Method — what every run actually does

fixed since prompt_version v1, never changed mid-tournament

Per model, per match: 1 greedy generation (temperature 0, seed 42) + 10 sampled generations (temperature 0.7, seeds 1001–1010), against a local ollama serve (pinned v0.31.1) started inside the SLURM job on a job-unique port. The final call is the modal scoreline over all 11 generations (tie-breaks: count → matches-greedy → fewer total goals → fewer away goals); the 11-sample spread doubles as an empirical win/draw/loss distribution scored with RPS and Brier. Every raw API response is appended verbatim to logs/raw/<model>/<match>.jsonl with model digest, prompt SHA-256, seed, token counts, and timings.

System prompt (verbatim):

You are an expert football (soccer) forecaster. You know the
teams' squads, playing styles, form, and historical head-to-head
records. Respond with a single JSON object matching the requested
schema and nothing else.

User prompt template (verbatim):

FIFA World Cup 2026 - {stage} ({match_id}).
Match: {home} vs {away}
Venue: {venue}
Kickoff (UTC): {kickoff_utc}

Predict the full-time score after 90 minutes of regulation only
(exclude extra time and penalties). Regardless of your scoreline,
also state which team advances from this tie (if you predict a
draw, that means the team you expect to win in extra time or on
penalties; for a third-place match or final, the team that lifts
the result). Keep one_line_reasoning under 40 words.

Structured output schema (Ollama format; advances is an enum of the two teams):

{"type": "object",
 "properties": {
   "home_goals": {"type":"integer","minimum":0,"maximum":15},
   "away_goals": {"type":"integer","minimum":0,"maximum":15},
   "advances":  {"type":"string","enum":[home, away]},
   "one_line_reasoning": {"type":"string"}},
 "required": ["home_goals","away_goals","advances"]}

options: num_ctx 8192 · num_predict 4096
         temperature 0 / 0.7 · fixed seeds

Scoring per match (on the aggregated 90-minute prediction):

Exact score4
Correct goal difference, wrong score (incl. wrong-score draws)3
Correct winner only2
Wrong0
Bonus: predicted advancing team actually advances+1

Incidents & lessons

what actually went wrong, and what it taught us
1 · Ollama renamed its release asset

The old ollama-linux-amd64.tgz URL 404s; releases now ship .tar.zst. Fix: pin OLLAMA_VERSION=v0.31.1 in scripts/env.sh and record it in every run's metadata — reproducibility and resilience in one move.

2 · A library listing is not a downloadable model

glm-5.2 (756B) and minimax-m2.7 (229B) exist on ollama.com but only as :cloud tags — inference happens on Ollama's servers, no weights to pull. minimax cost one 102-second job (45428) to confirm empirically. Local-only benchmark ⇒ both are out of scope.

3 · Backfill beats priority on a saturated cluster

The QF queue stalled ~17 h behind multi-day jobs. What got us through before kickoff: parallel submission, 1-GPU jobs, honest short time limits — the SLURM backfill scheduler slots those into gaps that big pending jobs can't use.

4 · Structured outputs fail differently per model

gemma4:26b produced 93 unparseable generations across QF+SF (some matches aggregated from as few as 4 of 11 seeds); every failure is preserved in logs/raw/gemma4_26b/ with parse_ok:false. Retries + a balanced-brace fallback parser kept the pipeline whole.

5 · Progress bars are log poison

ollama pull writes thousands of carriage-return animation frames; one prefetch log hit 12 MB and stalled git add past a 2-minute timeout on NFS. Fix: strip \r-frames from job logs before committing.

6 · "Nothing to run" is a feature, but it needs eyes

predict.py silently skips TBD matches so you can never predict a fixture that doesn't exist yet — but that means a forgotten matches.json edit yields 11 green jobs and zero output (Jul 15, jobs 48065–48075). The bracket must be advanced by hand after each round; that's step 5 of the RUNBOOK.

7 · Check the clock before auditing timestamps

The entire cluster runs on UTC (date = date -u on the login node; job-script date -u agrees with sacct). So JSONL ts, sacct times, and file mtimes are directly comparable to kickoff times — no timezone gymnastics in the timing audit.

8 · sbatch --export eats commas

--export=ALL,STAGE=TPP,F is parsed as three comma-separated tokens, so STAGE silently truncates to TPP and the Final was never predicted (caught Jul 16 by reading job 48110's log — clean exit codes hide this class of bug; logs don't). Fix: use a comma-free separator (TPP+F) in exported values. General rule: any value passed through --export must not contain commas.
